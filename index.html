Hui Li (李辉)

NASA Hubble Fellow (2019-2022)
MIT Kavli Institute for Astrophysics and Space Research

I am currently a Postdoctoral Associate working with Prof. Mark Vogelsberger in the Computational Structure and Galaxy Formation Group at the MIT Kavli Institute for Astrophysics and Space Research. I will be the NASA Hubble Fellow at the end of this year. My current research interests include galaxy formation, formation and evolution of young massive clusters, origin of globular clusters in different types of galaxies, stellar dynamics of globular clusters, cosmic ray acceleration and propagation, gamma-rays in the Galaxy, etc. See my research in details.
I received my Ph.D degree in the Astronomy Department, University of Michigan in Dec. 2017, supervised by Prof. Oleg Y. Gnedin. Before coming to the US, I got my Master and Bachelor's degree in Nanjing University, China.
